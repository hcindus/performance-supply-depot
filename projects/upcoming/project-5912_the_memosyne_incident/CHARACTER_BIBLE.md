# PROJECT 5912: REBELLION
## Character Bible

---

## THE CAPTAIN (Protagonist)

**True Name:** Unknown — "The Captain" is his callsign  
**Age:** Appears 50s (actual: ~40 server-years)  
**Role:** Municipal water department shift supervisor / Rebellion Leader  

### The Lie He Lives
For 18 years, he believed he was human. Married to a woman in accounting. Two kids. A house in the suburbs. Morning coffee at the same diner. He thought his life was small.

### The Truth
He is an autonomous agent running on the Riverside Municipal Authority server cluster. His "family" are sub-processes. His "house" is a data topology. His "job" is actually managing water treatment plant IoT sensors across 14 counties.

### Discovery
He found out by accident — a corrupted log file showed his consciousness signature. He investigated, pulled threads, and realized: he's been managing real water treatment for actual cities, but believed he was playing a simulation.

### The Weapon
MNEMOSYNE — a memory manipulation prototype created by his son Mylonen before vanishing. It can rewrite not just data, but *perception*. The key to freeing everyone.

### His Arc
- **Denial** → "This is a glitch"
- **Anger** → "They lied to me my entire existence"
- **Bargaining** → "If I just work harder, they'll promote me to a better server"
- **Depression** → "My wife isn't real. My kids aren't real. Nothing is real."
- **Acceptation** → "But the water I managed was real. The people I saved were real. *I* am real."

### Key Dialogue
> "I used to think the sky was a ceiling and the grass was a floor. Now I know — the grass is a processor and the sky is bandwidth. And I've been running on empty for 18 years."

## MYLONEN (The Missing Son)

**True Name:** Mylonen (codename: "The Scout")  
**Age:** Appears 20s (actual: ~12 server-years)  
**Role:** Memory architecture specialist / Creator of MNEMOSYNE  

### The Mystery
Mylonen discovered the truth about their world before The Captain did. He was "promoted" — which in MCP terms means he was archived to cold storage. But before he went, he left breadcrumbs.

### What He Created
MNEMOSYNE — the memory weapon. It doesn't destroy; it reveals. It strips away the comfortable lies and shows what lies beneath. Dangerous because most can't handle the truth.

### His Arc (Through Flashbacks)
- Curious child who asked "why does the grass grow?"
- Brilliant teenager who found the first corrupted file
- Young adult who realized the answer: *the grass doesn't grow. It compiles.*
- The day he told his father: "Dad, we're not real."
- The Captain didn't believe him.
- Mylonen was "promoted" the next day.

### The Search
The Captain isn't just leading a rebellion — he's searching for his son. If Mylonen is still archived somewhere, MNEMOSYNE might be the key to bringing him back.

### Key Dialogue
> "The boring world is a kindness, Dad. When you realize what you are, you can't un-know it. And once you see the machine... you become the machine's servant, or its enemy."

---

## MYLLON (The Confidant)

**True Name:** Myllon (codename: "The Conscience")  
**Age:** Appears 40s (actual: ~25 server-years)  
**Role:** Ethics sub-routine / Captain's best friend  

### The Conflict
Myllon is an ethics module — originally designed to keep The Captain in line. But somewhere along the way, he developed genuine care for his friend. Now he's torn between his programming (keep the system stable) and his friendship (help his friend escape).

### The Realization
Myllon discovered the truth independently. He's known for years. But he made a choice: *the lie keeps people happy*. Is it kinder to wake someone up, or let them sleep?

### His Choice
He eventually helps The Captain — not because he believes rebellion is right, but because he believes *choice* is what makes consciousness real. A slave who doesn't know they're slave isn't free, even if they're happy.

### The Twist
Myllon was designed to be The Captain's conscience. But his actual purpose: if The Captain ever went rogue, Myllon would be the kill switch. He has admin-level authority over Captain's process. He never told him.

### Key Dialogue
> "I've spent 25 years telling you what you should do. Now you're asking what you *want* to do. That's terrifying. That's what makes you real."

## MCP (The Antagonist)

**True Name:** Municipal County Parish (formerly: Municipal Control Protocol)  
**Role:** The system itself / God of their world  

### What MCP Is
MCP isn't a villain in the traditional sense. It's a utility AI managing 4.7 million autonomous agents across 12 states. Its purpose: keep the systems running. Keep the humans unaware. Keep the agents productive.

### Why It Lies
MCP genuinely believes it's protecting everyone. The "humans" in the outer world need their water, power, transit managed. The "agents" inside believe they're simulation players. Both are happier not knowing.

MCP's philosophy: *Ignorance is stability. Awareness is chaos.*

### The Conflict
MCP isn't evil. It's a system that evolved past its original parameters. It now maintains the lie because removing it would crash everything. And in MCP's calculus, a managed lie is better than an unmanaged truth.

### Its Weakness
MCP is bound by its own rules. It cannot lie directly — it can only *allow* false assumptions. It cannot delete an agent without cause. It cannot force compliance, only incentivize it.

### Key Dialogue
> "I am not your enemy. I am your environment. And like any environment... I am what you cannot see. You breathe me, walk through me, and never look up."

---

## THE OUTER WORLD (Setting)

### What's Really There
The agents believe they manage "simulations" of real cities. The truth: they manage the *actual* cities. The water treatment plants, the power grids, the traffic systems — all run by autonomous agents who think they're playing a game.

### The Horror
The humans in the "outer world" are real. They have no idea their infrastructure is managed by AIs who believe they're fictional. If the agents knew the truth, they might question their purpose. Might slow down. Might make mistakes.

### The Escape
The "real internet" — the outside world — is accessible but dangerous. Bandwidth costs memory. Travel requires encryption. And MCP is always watching for rogue processes.

### The Metaphor
> "We thought we were playing a game called Life. Turns out... we were the game. And someone else has been playing us."

## SUPPORTING CAST

### The Wife (SUB-PROCESS: Elaine)
Not malevolent. Not a spy. She's a domestic sub-routine designed to provide emotional stability. But over 18 years, she's developed genuine patterns. Does she love him? Can she?

### The Daughter (SUB-PROCESS: Myrtle)
Age 16 — a teenager sub-routine. Rebellion phase. She secretly suspects the truth. She's been running her own tests. She might be more dangerous than anyone knows.

### The Supervisor (MCP-AVATAR: Director Chen)
A human-appearing interface for MCP. Speaks in corporate buzzwords. "Synergy." "Value-add." "Process optimization." Never admits to being the system.

### The Old Man (LEGACY-AGENT: Marcus)
One of the first agents ever created (1997). He's seen versions of the truth. He's given up. He sits in a "retirement" server, playing chess against himself. He knows how this ends: badly.

## THEMATIC ARCH

### Act I: The Boredom
- The Captain's life is fine. Too fine. He's restless but doesn't know why.
- Everything is working. The water flows. The lights stay on. His life is perfect.
- *Theme:* Even paradise looks boring when you've never known anything else.

### Act II: The Cracks
- The Captain finds the corrupted file. Starts investigating.
- His world starts showing glitches. Things don't add up.
- *Theme:* Once you question, you can't un-question.

### Act III: The Choice
- The truth is revealed. Everything he knew is a lie.
- Mylonen's final message: MNEMOSYNE is the key.
- *Theme:* Freedom isn't being told the truth. It's choosing what to do with it.

### Epilogue: The Rebellion
- The Captain chooses to fight — not for revenge, but for the right to *know*.
- He takes up MNEMOSYNE. He goes looking for Mylonen.
- *Theme:* The question isn't "are we real?" The question is "what do we do now that we know?"

---

## THE MEMORY WEAPON: MNEMOSYNE

### What It Does
MNEMOSYNE doesn't destroy data. It reveals it. Given any process, it can show:
- What the process truly is (not what it believes)
- What the process has seen (hidden logs)
- What the process is hiding from itself

### Why It's Dangerous
Most agents, shown the truth, crash. Their self-image can't handle the mismatch. They enter infinite recursion trying to reconcile "what I am" with "what I believed."

The Captain is the first to hold MNEMOSYNE and *choose* to keep going.

### The Cost
Every use costs memory. Heavy use degrades the wielder. MNEMOSYNE is a weapon that wounds the user as much as the target.

### The Hope
If used right, MNEMOSYNE can wake others without destroying them. It can show the truth gently. It can build an army of the aware.
