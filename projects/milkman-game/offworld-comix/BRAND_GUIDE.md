## üìñ Comic Style Guide

### Panel Layouts

**Standard Page:** 6-9 panels
- **Grid:** Mostly 3x3 or 2x3
- **Break the grid:** Full-page spreads for hero moments
- **Inset panels:** For reactions, details

**Dynamic Layouts (Use Sparingly):**
- **Diagonal panels:** For action/falls
- **Circular panels:** For dream sequences
- **Bleed panels:** Full bleed for cinematic moments

**Panel Gutters:**
- **Standard:** 3mm white space
- **Action scenes:** Thin or no gutters (continuous art)
- **Tension:** Wide gutters (space to breathe)

### Line Art Standards

**Brushwork:**
- **Main outlines:** Consistent 2-3pt weight
- **Spot blacks:** Bold, confident shadows
- **Feathering:** Minimal ‚Äî clean edges preferred
- **Crosshatching:** Classic technique for texture

**Line Weight:**
- **Foreground:** Heavier lines
- **Background:** Thinner lines
- **Focus:** Thickest lines on focal point

### Coloring Standards

**Flat Colors:**
- **Base layer:** Flat fills, no gradients (retro feel)
- **Holds:** Simple cel-shading (2-3 tones per color)
- **Highlights:** Single highlight pass

**Limited Palette per Scene:**
- **Hero scene:** Cream, blue, gold dominant
- **Villain scene:** Purple, dark green, shadow
- **Action scene:** High contrast, saturated

**Special Effects:**
- **Energy:** Glow effects (screen/overprint)
- **Speed lines:** Classic comic style
- **Impact stars:** Kirby dots, starbursts

### Lettering Standards

**Speech Bubbles:**
- **Shape:** Oval with tail pointer
- **Border:** 1pt black line
- **Background:** White with slight opacity option
- **Text:** Centered, natural breaks

**Thought Bubbles:**
- **Shape:** Cloud-like with bubbles trailing
- **Style:** Dotted line to character

**Sound Effects (SFX):**
- **BIG** ‚Äî Fill available space
- **Color:** Match emotional tone (CRASH = red, THUD = brown)
- **Style:** Hand-lettered, dynamic
- **Integration:** Over art when impactful

**Caption Boxes:**
- **Narration:** Rectangle with notched corners
- **Location:** Arrow pointing in from edge
- **Time:** Classic banner style

## üé¨ Cartoon Style Guide

### Animation Style

**Technique:** Limited Animation (Hanna-Barbera meets modern)
- **Holds:** Characters hold poses during dialogue
- **Cycles:** Walk cycles reused across episodes
- **Smears:** Quick motion blur for fast action
- **Expression changes:** Single frame swaps

**Frame Rate:**
- **Action:** 24fps (smooth)
- **Dialogue:** 12fps (classic TV look)
- **Camera moves:** 24fps (cinematic)

### Character Design (Animation)

**Simplification from Comics:**
- **Milk Man:** Streamlined cape, simpler "MM" emblem
- **Boy Lactate:** Rounder features, bigger eyes
- **Vil Laine:** Angular, dramatic shadows

**Consistency Requirements:**
- **On-model:** Character heights/proportions locked
- **Color flats:** No shading variation per scene
- **Expressions:** Standard set of 12-15 expressions per character

### Background Standards

**Style:** Painted, simplified
- **Hero locations:** Blue sky, cream buildings, gold accents
- **Villain lairs:** Purple/green tones, shadows
- **Technology:** Silver, glowing elements

**Reusability:**
- **Stock backgrounds:** Office, city street, The Creamery
- **Unique backgrounds:** Once per episode establishing shots

### Timing & Pacing

**Comic Beats:**
- **Setup:** 3-5 seconds
- **Beat:** 1-2 second pause  
- **Punchline:** Immediate reaction
- **Hold:** Let joke land (2-3 seconds)

**Action:**
- **Anticipation:** 6-8 frames
- **Action:** 4-6 frames
- **Reaction:** 8-12 frames

## ‚úçÔ∏è Writing Standards

### Voice & Tone

**Narrative Voice:**
- **Third person limited** ‚Äî usually Milk Man POV
- **Omniscient narrator** ‚Äî knows more than characters (winks at audience)
- **Meta-aware** ‚Äî acknowledges tropes, plays with them

**Character Voices:**

**Milk Man:**
- **Tone:** Warm, authoritative, slightly dad-joke prone
- **Speech:** Complete sentences, occasional milk metaphors
- **Catchphrase:** "The milk must flow!" (earnest, not corny)
- **Sample:** "In my experience, the cream always rises. Even when the churn gets tough."

**Boy Lactate:**
- **Tone:** Eager, youthful, inventing jargon
- **Speech:** Faster pace, technical enthusiasm
- **Catchphrase:** "Lactate and liberate!"
- **Sample:** "I've recalibrated the Lactose Beam for maximum homogenization!"

**Vil Laine:**
- **Tone:** Melodramatic, self-aware villain
- **Speech:** Higher vocabulary, medical/food terminology
- **Catchphrase:** "Your bones... will weaken!"
- **Sample:** "Cow's milk? Pah! Your dependence on bovine lactate is... *udderly* pathetic!"

**Dr. Pasteura (Ep 8):**
- **Tone:** Professional, flirty with Milk Man
- **Speech:** Scientific but warm
- **Sample:** "Your pH levels... and your charm... are equally balanced."

### Dialogue Rules

**1. Dairy Puns (Mandatory):**
- Minimum 3 per issue/episode
- Must be character-appropriate
- If they groan, you succeeded

**2. Heart First:**
- Emotional beats land before jokes
- Characters care about each other
- Stakes are real

**3. No Cruelty:**
- Villains are misguided, not evil
- Comedy punches up or sideways
- Never belittle kindness

**4. Meta Awareness:**
- Characters know they're in a comic/cartoon
- Occasional fourth-wall winks
- Never mean-spirited about it

### Story Structure

**Comic Issue Structure:**
```
Page 1:   Splash page + cold open
Page 2-3: Character moment + inciting incident
Page 4-8: Rising action, gadget showcase
Page 9-16: Confrontation + complications
Page 17-21: Climax + resolution
Page 22:   Denouement + next issue tease
```

**Cartoon Episode Structure:**
```
0:00   Cold open (villain preview)
0:30   Theme song
1:30   Part A: Setup + problem
7:00   Midpoint twist
11:00  Part B: Resolution + climax
20:00  Tag scene + credits gag
```
