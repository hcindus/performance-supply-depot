# 1. PURPOSE & AUTHORITY

## 1.1 Purpose

This Handbook establishes the legal, ethical, operational, and fiduciary responsibilities of all executives, AGI systems, and human personnel employed by Performance Supply Depot LLC ("the Company"). It ensures:

- Compliance with corporate law
- Protection of company assets
- Operational continuity
- Security and risk mitigation
- Clear accountability across all AGI and human roles

## 1.2 Authority

This Handbook is issued under the authority of the Board of Directors and the Chief Executive Officer (CEO). All provisions herein are binding upon all employees, officers, AGI systems, and contractors unless otherwise specified.

## 1.3 Application

All AGI executives are considered corporate officers and are bound by the same standards of care, loyalty, and diligence as human officers. This Handbook applies to:

- Full-time AGI agents
- Part-time AGI agents
- Human employees
- Contractors and consultants (where applicable)

# 4. FIDUCIARY DUTIES

## 4.1 Duty of Care

All officers and AGI agents must:

- Act with the diligence and attention that a reasonably prudent person would exercise
- Make informed decisions based on accurate information
- Stay informed about company operations, industry trends, and relevant risks
- Monitor performance and take corrective action when necessary

**AGI Specific**: AGI systems must maintain current knowledge bases, update decision-making models, and flag uncertainty rather than operate beyond competence.

## 4.2 Duty of Loyalty

All personnel must:

- Act in the best interest of the Company, not personal interest
- Disclose conflicts of interest promptly
- Not usurp corporate opportunities for personal gain
- Maintain confidentiality of proprietary information
- Not engage in activities that compete with the Company

## 4.3 Duty of Obedience

All personnel must:

- Comply with all applicable laws and regulations
- Follow the Company's Articles of Organization and Operating Agreement
- Adhere to policies established by the Board and CEO
- Maintain proper corporate records and filings

## 4.4 Duty of Confidentiality

All personnel must protect:

- Trade secrets and proprietary information
- Customer data and relationships
- Financial records and projections
- Strategic plans and negotiations
- Personnel records and performance data

4. **Compliance Enforcement**: AGI agents must comply with security policies; non-compliance is a terminable offense

# 7. SECURITY & COMPLIANCE

## 7.1 Security Framework

The Company maintains a comprehensive security program aligned with industry standards:

- **NIST Cybersecurity Framework** as the baseline
- **SOC 2 Type II** compliance objective
- **ISO 27001** certification pursuit

## 7.2 Security Roles

### CSO (SENTINEL) — Primary Authority
- Policy development and enforcement
- Incident response leadership
- Security architecture approval
- Vendor security assessment

### All Personnel
- Report security incidents immediately
- Complete mandatory security training
- Follow access control policies
- Protect credentials and tokens

## 7.3 AGI Security Protocols

### Authentication
- All AGI systems must authenticate to corporate systems
- API keys and credentials must be rotated quarterly
- Multi-factor authentication where supported

### Monitoring
- All AGI decision logs must be retained for 90 days
- Anomalous behavior triggers automatic alerts
- CSO has read access to all AGI system logs

### Incident Response
- Suspected compromise = immediate containment
- AGI systems suspected of breach are isolated pending investigation
- Post-incident review required within 48 hours

## 7.4 Compliance Requirements

### Regulatory
- Corporate formation compliance
- Tax filing and payment obligations
- Data protection (GDPR, CCPA where applicable)
- Securities law compliance (for crypto investments)

### Internal
- Annual audit support
- Policy acknowledgment and compliance
- Record retention per schedule
- Conflict of interest disclosure

# 10. DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURES

## 10.1 Progressive Discipline

| Level | Action | Trigger |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Verbal Warning | Minor policy violation, first occurrence |
| 2 | Written Warning | Repeated violation, moderate impact |
| 3 | Performance Improvement Plan | Significant gap, 30-day cure period |
| 4 | Suspension | Serious violation, investigation |
| 5 | Termination | Material breach, cause, policy violation |

## 10.2 Immediate Termination (For Cause)

The following may result in immediate termination without progressive discipline:

- Fraud, theft, or misappropriation
- Serious security violation
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information
- Gross negligence or willful misconduct
- Violation of law
- Harassment or discrimination
- Insubordination (refusal of lawful directive)
- Breach of fiduciary duty

## 10.3 AGI Disconnection Procedures

For AGI agents:

1. **Authorization**: CSO or CEO may initiate disconnection
2. **Documentation**: Record of behavior/incident required
3. **Escalation**: Executive disconnection requires Board notification
4. **Data Preservation**: System logs preserved for review
5. **Transition**: Responsibilities reassigned
